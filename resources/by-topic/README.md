## Practical Integration Examples

### 1. Role Definition Alignment

**Government of Canada** suggests a 7-role model including "Administration."

**Internal Implementation**: Combines administrative functions into:
- **Content Authoring** (documentation, content strategy)
- **Business Analysis** (requirements, compliance, project management)

**Rationale**: Government projects often have separate administration roles that don't directly impact accessibility implementation.

### 2. Design Role Expansion

**Hassell Inclusion** focuses on visual design and UX.

**Internal Implementation**: Splits "Design" into:
- **Visual Design** (contrast, visual hierarchy, color theory)
- **User Experience Design** (user research, interaction patterns, accessibility testing)

**Rationale**: Accessibility requires both visual and user experience considerations, with specific technical constraints.

### 3. Development Role Integration

**Deque University** focuses on frontend development.

**Internal Implementation**: Expands to include:
- **Frontend Development** (HTML, CSS, JavaScript)
- **Testing** (validation, verification, evidence collection)

**Rationale**: Accessibility cannot be separated from the complete development lifecycle.

## Evolution of Role-Based Accessibility

### **Traditional Approach:**
- Single-role responsibility (developer handles all accessibility)
- Reactive bug-fixing focus
- Compliance-driven checklist approach

### **Role-Based Approach (This Repository):**
- **Specialized roles** with distinct responsibilities
- **Proactive collaboration** across expertise areas
- **Evidence-based decision making** with clear accountability
- **Integration of accessibility** into every phase of development

### **Future Trends:**
1. **Cross-organizational collaboration** - External partnerships
2. **AI-assisted role analysis** - Automated role identification
3. **Role-based career paths** - Specialized accessibility roles
4. **Global role standards** - International role harmonization
